abstract: "This paper explores the quadratic variation (QV) as an alternative measure to the bid-ask spread in limit order markets when observed at high resolution. Although the spread cannot be precisely estimated because of microstructure noise, the QV of the price series in limit order markets is an important property of high-frequency datasets. An empirical examination of a sample of stocks from the NASDAQ 100 is used to estimate and examine the QV at subsecond timescale. The QV of trades (29.73E–05) is distant from the QV measured with pretrade and posttrade price series. The measure called the limit-to-market order signal shows a consistent pattern of decline in stocks with increasing activity."
keywords: "High resolution, Limit order markets, Market microstructure, Market quality, Quadratic variation"
