abstract: "This paper examines the dynamics of house prices in metropolitan cities in an emerging economy, exploring spatial heterogeneity using data for 35 Indian cities. The research methodology uses panel econometrics allowing for spatial heterogeneity, cross-sectional dependence and non-stationary data. Findings suggest that long-term fundamentals drive the Indian housing markets, where wealth parameters are stronger than supply-side parameters. The research documents in an emerging market context the observations cited in low-leverage developed markets such as Germany and Japan."
